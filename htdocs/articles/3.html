Why taking regular and small breaks between coding time is necassary?

You keep reading that it is recommended for a programmer to take frequent breaks while programming, and the usual recommendation you see is 5 minutes every half hour or 10 minutes every hour.

You gave it a try, but quite often you find something interesting during those 5 minutes, and it takes me away from what you was working on for longer than you planned. Either that, or your mind gets focused on something else and you find it hard to get back into your work and don't focus very well

You do take frequent breaks but they normally have a purpose (bathroom, food/coffee, etc). You tend to find that while you are away from your desk that you are still thinking of the problem at hand. However, this thinking is not distracted by the code in front of you and allows you to think more about the problem as a whole rather than nitpicking through details in front of you. Frequently when you return to your desk you have a new idea at how to approach the issue you are working on.
